ROOSTIFY

PRESS RELEASE

Deliver a Better Credit Experience to Ease Lender Stress

Background

Home mortgage is painful. Not only for borrowers, but also for lenders who work through the mounds of paperwork. As part of the process, the credit inquiry is one of the foundational pieces of the loan process. However, the current credit experience is being challenged by the disparated workflow, manual documentation process, and poor communication with borrowers.

With major banking clients such as JP Morgan Chase, TD Bank, HSBC, etc, Roostify is reinventing the home mortgage experience through a streamlined and automated digital mortgage platform.

Our goal is to ease lender stress by providing a more integrated, guided and collaborative credit experience, and ultimately, accelerate loan approval.

My Role

I am leading the design of the credit service including user research, prototyping and visual design. I am collaborating with a product manager, an engineer team in delivering the credit service, alongside the design team in refining the design system that is related to this project.

The product was released in June 2020.

Research

Our Users

In the mortgage process, the credit check is initiated and completed by the lenders. As a result, we focused on leveraging the credit experience for lenders. However, smoothing out the borrower-facing steps can significantly accelerate the process and build the relationship between lenders and borrowers.

Insights from the Loan Officers

We interviewed 5 loan officers around their experience pulling credit. Our goals were to understand their current process and the challenges they face.

Reading of the credit report is frustrating

The current credit report for lenders is long-winded and frustrating,causing the loan officers to often miss the details they want to catch or misinterpret the information.

Follow ups surrounding a credit check is intense

Credit score is not the only deciding factor for loan approval. Ancillary follow-up actions and additional communication can be a burden to loan officers due to the manual processing.

Repeating keying data

The loan officers constantly switch between multiple systems to perform various tasks. Repeating keying data results in hard stops in their workflow.

Framing the problem

It takes more than a look at the score

Seeing a 720+ score is definitely the most exciting moment for lenders. However, most borrowers do not have, or are close to the satisfying score. Scrutinizing every single line of credit, requesting documents from borrowers, handing off data to the downstream can cause stress, anxiety and uncertainty in lenders’ workflow.
Understanding the major pain for lenders, I proposed the following goals to guide the design:

Introducing Roostify Credit Service

Bringing More Focus on What's Important

Say Goodbuy to the dated PDF. The new credit report brings a simplified view with clarify and focus. The most critical information is brought to the front while the full details is available when necessary.

Collaborating on tasks

Lenders relied on emails to communicate with borrowers, which resulted in slow processing and anxiety for borrowers. The new credit service allows lenders and borrowers to collaborate on the same task without manually drafting emails. In the meantime, borrowers can worry less and feel supported.

Easily update debts to improve loan approval

For the borrowers who are very close to qualify, the lenders will help borrowers improve their creditr profile by reducing their amount of the debt. The loan officers now have the ability to easily update the debt and allow the underwriters in the downstream to be aware of the changes.

Review Anytime, Anywhere

Loan officers often get last minute requests from borrowers, during evenings and weekends. We understand the circumstances and give loan officers time back by allowing rapid retrieval of the credit information while they are often distracted.

Design Process

How I GOT THERE

Ease the pain of reading

Understanding what loan officers are dealing with is important to build the context. The existing credit reports (lender version) remain the PDF manner as years ago. The poorly structured content and a lack of visual focus bury the key information beneath. To validate our assumptions , we had a semi-structured interview with loan officers to understand what is important essentially for them to complete their work.

Current Credit Report

Bring the holistic view to the front

Aiming to bring a focus and leave the niggling details behind, I restructured the content into 4 sections that are essential to loan officers’ decision making. The tabs design was introduced to offer a full picture of the borrower’s credit history instantly, and to adapt to their reading habit: dig into the very bottom of the information when necessary.

Design Iterations

Through out the project, more feedback was received and requirements changed. Early on I learnt that the reading is a pain, so I decided to hide the unecessary details and add more visual delightments. As we received more feedback, the problem shifted from how much information we should hide for a ease of reading, to what's central to loan officers' daily tasks.

Iterations of tabs

Borrower Filter

One intersting finding we have discovered when solving this problem is:
If one of the borrowers showcases a really bad credit and is dragging down the overall credit score, the loan officers might remove the borrower from the application in order to improve the chance of loan approval.
This finding indicated the feature of borrower filter. The loan officers can toggle off one of the borrowers to view their invidual monthly debts and other credit information. As a result, the loan officers can run the scenario with one click so as to make better decisions for this loan.

Borrower Filter

Responsive Layout

Considering the variety of our clients, it is important to scale the design to different working scenarios: Scrutinizing line by line through a computer in the bank, or a quick review during transit. The figma contraint allows me to play around with how all the elements scale within one breakpoint.

Streamline the follow ups

Getting the credit report only opens the initial conversation with borrowers. there are lot of work that need to be done. Whenever there are things that need explanation, for example, why this borrower had this late payment last year. the loan officers will always request a letter of explanation. This is to help eliminate any potential problems or improve the credit before this application is sent to final underwriting.

Automate the manual steps

A lot of time is wasted in manually drafting emails and communicating back and forth with borrowers. We want to free the loan officers from the manual steps and in the meantime, reduce the anxiety from the borrower side.

New LOE flow

Based on our conversations with the LOs, I proposed a new user flow that tackles the manual processing:

1. Once the credit has been pulled, the following will be identified to generate a letter of explanation: Recent Inquiries, Derogatory marks and High Balance.

2. Lenders can prefill the letter on behalf of the borrower after they have a conversation about all the issues on the credit report.

3. Borrowers will only need to review and sign and letter.
